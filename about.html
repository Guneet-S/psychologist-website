Hello, I'm Dr. Kiran Deep Kaur

I am a child and adolescent psychologist with over 15 years of experience working with children, young people, and their families in Chandigarh, India. My practice is built on the belief that every child — regardless of their abilities or challenges — has the potential to flourish.

My journey into psychology began with a deep curiosity about how children develop, learn, and make sense of the world around them. Over the years, I have had the privilege of working with a wide range of children, including those with ADHD, intellectual disabilities, visual impairments, and other special needs.

"I believe the most powerful thing we can offer a struggling child is a space where they feel truly seen, heard, and safe." — Dr. Kiran Deep Kaur

I trained at Panjab University, Chandigarh, and have since worked in schools, hospitals, and community clinics across the region. I work collaboratively with parents, teachers, and other professionals to create wraparound support that extends beyond the therapy room.

I bring specialist training in visual impairment through my Special B.Ed qualification, allowing me to offer genuinely inclusive psychological support that is adapted for children with sensory needs. My approach is always warm, evidence-based, and child-led.

My approach

What I believe in

Three core values that guide every session and every relationship with children and families.

Compassion First

Every session begins with empathy. I create a non-judgmental, warm space where children feel safe to express themselves without fear.

Evidence-Based

I use only well-researched, proven therapeutic approaches — including CBT, play therapy, and behavioural interventions — adapted for each child.

Family-Centred

Families are central to a child's progress. I work closely with parents and caregivers as partners throughout the entire therapeutic journey.

How I work

My therapeutic approach

No two children are the same. I draw on a range of evidence-based methods, carefully adapting my approach to the individual child, their developmental stage, and their family context.

  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)

    Helping children identify and change unhelpful thought patterns and behaviours in a structured, goal-oriented way that builds long-lasting skills.

  • Play Therapy

    Using play as a natural language for younger children to process emotions and experiences in a safe, expressive, child-led environment.

  • Behavioural Interventions

    Practical, structured strategies for managing challenging behaviours, particularly for children with ADHD and intellectual disabilities.

  • Parent Coaching

    Equipping parents with understanding, skills, and confidence to support their child's growth between sessions and at home.

  • Psychoeducational Assessment

    Comprehensive assessments to identify a child's strengths, difficulties, and learning profile, with clear recommendations for home and school.
