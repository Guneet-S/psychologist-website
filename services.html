Core Service

Child & Adolescent Psychology

I offer comprehensive psychological support for children aged 4 to 18 years and teenagers facing emotional, social, or behavioural difficulties. My approach is always tailored to the individual child's age, personality, and specific needs.

Whether your child is struggling with anxiety, low self-esteem, school difficulties, friendship problems, or emotional regulation, I can help them develop the skills and confidence to navigate life more effectively.

Who is this for? Children and teenagers aged 4–18 experiencing anxiety, depression, low self-esteem, school refusal, emotional outbursts, social difficulties, grief, or general wellbeing concerns.
  • Initial assessment and detailed case formulation
  • Individual therapy sessions (50 minutes)
  • Cognitive Behavioural Therapy (CBT)
  • Play therapy for younger children
  • Parent consultation and coaching
  • School liaison (with consent)

Special Needs

Support for Visually Impaired Children

Children with visual impairments face unique psychological challenges — from developing independence and confidence to navigating social situations and managing any associated frustration or grief. I provide specialised psychological support that acknowledges and celebrates the whole child.

My work in this area is adapted to be fully accessible, using tactile, auditory, and other sensory modalities to engage each child meaningfully in therapy.

Who is this for? Children with partial or complete visual impairment, including those with congenital or acquired vision loss, and their families.
  • Psychological assessment adapted for visual impairment
  • Emotional support and resilience building
  • Self-concept and identity development
  • Anxiety management strategies
  • Parent and caregiver guidance
  • Collaboration with vision specialists and teachers

Special Needs

ADHD Assessment & Support

Attention Deficit Hyperactivity Disorder affects many children and can have a significant impact on learning, relationships, and self-esteem. I offer comprehensive ADHD assessments and evidence-based therapeutic support for children and their families.

I work closely with parents to develop practical strategies for home and school environments, and can provide detailed reports to support access to accommodations.

Who is this for? Children showing signs of inattention, hyperactivity, or impulsivity; children already diagnosed with ADHD who need therapeutic support; parents seeking clarity and strategies.
  • Comprehensive ADHD assessment (multi-session)
  • Detailed written report with recommendations
  • Behavioural intervention planning
  • Executive function skills coaching
  • Parent training and behavioural strategies
  • School consultation and IEP support

Special Needs

Intellectual Disability Support

Children with intellectual disabilities deserve high-quality psychological support that respects their individuality and maximises their potential. I provide assessments and therapy using adapted, accessible approaches that meet each child where they are.

My work is always strengths-based — I focus on what children can do and help families build on those foundations.

Who is this for? Children with mild to moderate intellectual disabilities, developmental delays, or those awaiting or following a formal diagnosis.
  • Psychoeducational and adaptive functioning assessment
  • Adapted therapy using visual aids and simplified language
  • Emotional regulation and social skills support
  • Behaviour management planning
  • Family support and caregiver training
  • Transition planning (school to adulthood)
